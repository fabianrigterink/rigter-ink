The Hunter Valley Coal Chain (HVCC) is one of the world's largest coal export supply chains, moving coal from mines in the Hunter Valley of New South Wales to the Port of Newcastle. Rail lines, load-out facilities, stockyards, and ship loaders all have finite capacity and require maintenance.

We build a simulation model of the HVCC to evaluate how maintenance capacity can be scheduled without degrading export throughput. The model captures train paths, stockpile buffers, ship arrival patterns, and the interactions between maintenance windows at different facilities. We use the model to identify bottlenecks and test capacity alignment strategies — scheduling maintenance at complementary times across facilities to avoid compound throughput losses.
