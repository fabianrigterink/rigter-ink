The McCormick relaxation of a bilinear term $xy$ on a box $[x^L, x^U] \times [y^L, y^U]$ is the convex hull of the graph of $xy$ restricted to that box — it is tight by construction. However, in practice the LP relaxation of a problem containing many bilinear terms is often much weaker than what the individual McCormick envelopes suggest, because the convex hull of the joint set of bilinear terms is smaller than the Cartesian product of individual envelopes.

We study this gap: how much weaker is the McCormick relaxation compared to the true convex hull of all bilinear terms together? We derive sharp bounds on this gap and characterise cases where the two coincide. The results quantify when McCormick envelopes are sufficient and when stronger techniques (such as RLT or extended formulations) are needed.
