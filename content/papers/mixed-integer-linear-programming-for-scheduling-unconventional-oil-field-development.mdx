Unconventional oil fields (shale, tight oil) are developed by drilling and completing many well pads in a sequence. The order matters: early pads generate cash flow that funds later ones, infrastructure (pipelines, processing facilities) has limited capacity, and rig availability constrains how many pads can be active at once.

We formulate the scheduling problem as a mixed-integer linear program (MILP). The model captures cash-flow dynamics, capacity constraints, and rig scheduling simultaneously. We demonstrate on realistic instances that the MILP finds substantially better development schedules than industry heuristics, with solution times suitable for practical planning horizons.
