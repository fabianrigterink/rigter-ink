The pooling problem asks how to blend raw materials flowing through a network of tanks (pools) to meet quality specifications at minimum cost. Despite its simple description, the problem is NP-hard and nonconvex due to the bilinear quality-balance constraints that arise when streams of different compositions mix in a pool.

This thesis makes several contributions:

- **New formulations.** I develop multi-commodity flow formulations that track the origin of each unit of flow through the network. These yield stronger LP relaxations than the classical $p$- and $q$-formulations, closing a significant portion of the optimality gap before any branching.

- **Polynomially solvable cases.** I identify graph-theoretic conditions under which the pooling problem can be solved in polynomial time, and provide exact algorithms for these cases.

- **Convex hull results.** Together with co-authors, I characterise the convex hull of the feasible region for certain substructures, leading to tighter relaxations and extended formulations.

- **Applications.** The results are applied to coal supply chains in the Hunter Valley and to generalised pooling problems arising in the mining industry, demonstrating practical impact on large-scale industrial instances.
