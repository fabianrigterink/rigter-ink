A recurring challenge in global optimisation of polynomial programs is computing tight convex relaxations of products $xy$ where $x \in [x^L, x^U]$ and $y \in [y^L, y^U]$. The standard McCormick relaxation is the convex hull in the original space, but it can be strengthened by lifting into a higher-dimensional space and projecting back.

We characterise the convex hull of the graph of $xy$ restricted to various combinatorial structures (e.g., when one variable is binary, or when the variables satisfy additional linear constraints) and derive explicit extended formulations — linear descriptions in the lifted space. These extended formulations are tight by construction and can be embedded directly into MILP/MINLP solvers via column generation or direct reformulation.
