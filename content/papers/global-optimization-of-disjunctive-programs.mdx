Disjunctive programs are optimisation problems where the feasible set is a union of convex sets — for example, $x \in P_1 \cup P_2 \cup \cdots \cup P_k$ where each $P_i$ is a polyhedron. They arise naturally in problems with logical conditions, complementarity constraints, or piecewise-linear objectives.

We develop a branch-and-bound algorithm that exploits disjunctive structure directly, without first converting to a MILP via big-$M$ or convex hull reformulations. At each node, we compute a lower bound by solving a convex relaxation over the disjunctive hull, and branch by selecting a disjunct to fix. We prove convergence to global optimality and demonstrate that the approach can outperform standard MILP reformulations when the number of disjuncts is small but the individual sets are complex.
