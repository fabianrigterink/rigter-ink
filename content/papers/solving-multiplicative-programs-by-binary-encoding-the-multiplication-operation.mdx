Multiplicative programs — optimisation problems whose objectives or constraints involve products of decision variables — are nonconvex and generally hard to solve globally. Standard linearisation techniques like McCormick envelopes produce weak relaxations when many variables interact.

We propose an exact reformulation that encodes each multiplication via binary expansion of one of its operands. This converts products into sums of terms involving binary variables, yielding a MILP that can be handed to standard solvers. The approach is particularly effective when one operand has bounded integer or near-integer values. We show correctness, derive bound tightening techniques, and compare against McCormick-based approaches on benchmark instances.
