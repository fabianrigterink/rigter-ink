The generalized pooling problem extends the classical pooling problem by allowing pools to feed other pools (creating nested blending structures). This arises in mining operations where crushed ore from multiple sources is blended in stages before reaching the output terminal.

We study a special case where the pool-to-pool topology has a specific structure motivated by the Hunter Valley coal export network. For this class, we derive polynomial-time algorithms and show that the LP relaxation of our formulation is exact (i.e., the LP optimal equals the global optimum). This result explains why practitioners using LP-based tools obtain globally optimal solutions on these instances without resorting to global optimisation solvers.
